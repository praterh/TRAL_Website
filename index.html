Ras Muhammad National Park

General

Ras Muhammad National Park is Egypt's very first national park. It encompasses an area of 480 square kilometers (around 185 square miles), with over half of that being area over water. Known for its coral reefs and diverse marine wildlife, it is a common bucket list location for divers and travelers alike.

Management

Ras Muhammad is managed by the Egyptian Environmental Affairs Agency (EEAA), but specifically by the EEAA's Department of Natural Reserves; this department oversees the management of all Egypt's national parks and protected areas. The EEAA is funded by the Environmental Protection Fund, which was established in 1994.
